Model-Based Designed Software Test Engineer at Supernal

In this role, I am developing emulators for the Honeywell Avionics line of products and those emulators are then fed into a "Functional Simulator" developed in Simulink. One might ask the question: "Why developing emulators when the company purchased real commercial sensors?". The answer is: "Fault injection". In simulation, real harware cannot be used, but it is possible to introduce faults, which would be impossible when using real-sensor. So instead, we emulate real-sensors using Simulink for the software, and QNX for the real-time operating system. Of course, the communication protocols and connectivity schemes are replicated. Aerospace protocols used:

  • ARINC 429
  • ARINC 664/AFDX
  • CAN
  • Ethernet UDP

Honeywell AHRS, IMU and RADALT Emulators (ARINC 429)
